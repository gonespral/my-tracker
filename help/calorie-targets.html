Calorie targets

How MyTracker sets your daily calorie goal and adjusts it on training days.

Your base target (rest day)

If you fill in your profile in Settings → Calories, MyTracker uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and then multiplies by an activity level factor to get your daily target.

BMR (male) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
BMR (female) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

Rest day target = BMR × activity level multiplier

If you have not filled in your profile, the target defaults to 2150 kcal on rest days and 1800 kcal BMR. You can also set a custom target directly in Settings.

Activity level multipliers

LevelMultiplierDescription
Sedentary 1.2xLittle to no movement
Light 1.375xLight daily movement
Moderate 1.55xTypical day with regular movement
Active 1.725xHigh movement or frequent training
Very active 1.9xVery active job or training volume

Training days

When you log a workout, MyTracker automatically adds the net calories burned to your target for that day. This keeps you fuelled for the work you actually did without you having to adjust anything manually.

Net active calories

The calories added on training days are net active calories, not the gross total from your workout. Your BMR keeps burning during exercise anyway, so only the extra burn on top of that is added.

420 kcal gross burn − 62 kcal BMR (45 min) = 358 kcal added

This prevents double-counting your resting metabolism. A 45-minute workout that burns 420 kcal only adds around 358 kcal to your target, because you would have burned 62 kcal just existing during those 45 minutes.

Duplicate workouts (the same activity imported from both Strava and Google Health on the same day) are detected and excluded from the net calories calculation so they do not inflate your target.

Macronutrient targets

Macro targets use a 30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat split applied to your calorie target. The gram values update automatically if your calorie target changes. You can see today's macro breakdown on the ring charts on the Today tab.

Protein

30% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 161 g

Carbohydrates

40% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 215 g

Fat

30% of calories ÷ 9 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 72 g
