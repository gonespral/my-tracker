Calorie targets

How MyTracker calculates your daily calorie goal from BMR, activity level, deficit, and exercise eat-back.

Your base target

Fill in your profile under Settings → Calories and MyTracker uses the Mifflin–St Jeor equation to estimate your Basal Metabolic Rate, then scales it by your activity level to get your maintenance TDEE.

BMR (male) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
BMR (female) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

Maintenance TDEE = BMR × activity level multiplier
Base goal = max(0, TDEE − deficit)

If you have not filled in your profile, the target defaults to 2150 kcal with no deficit. You can also set a custom fixed target directly in Settings.

Activity level multipliers

LevelMultiplierDescription
Sedentary 1.2×Little to no movement outside of workouts
Light 1.375×Light daily movement
Moderate 1.55×Typical day with regular movement
Active 1.725×High movement or frequent training
Very active 1.9×Very active job or training volume

Exercise eat-back

When you log a workout, MyTracker can raise your calorie target for that day by a percentage of the active calories you burned. This is the eat-back. It is on by default at 50% and can be adjusted or disabled in Settings → Calories → Activity eat-back.

420 kcal total burn − 62 kcal resting (45 min × BMR rate) = 358 kcal active

The resting portion is subtracted because your TDEE already accounts for calories burned at rest. Only the extra burn above that baseline counts. MyTracker always works in active calories: Strava imports are converted on the way in (total minus resting burn for the activity duration), Google Health and manually logged workouts report active calories directly. At 50% eat-back, 179 kcal would be added to your target for that day.

When eat-back is enabled, your activity level in Settings is automatically locked to Sedentary. This prevents double-counting: active-day burn is captured through eat-back from logged workouts rather than baked into your base TDEE multiplier.

Deficit

A deficit is subtracted from your maintenance TDEE to produce the base goal. It applies every day, regardless of whether you exercise. Eat-back is then added on top of that reduced base on days you log a workout.

For example: 2150 kcal TDEE with a 300 kcal deficit gives a 1850 kcal base. On a day with 358 net active kcal burned at 50% eat-back, the effective target is 1850 + 179 = 2029 kcal.

Duplicate workouts — the same activity imported from both Strava and Google Health — are detected and excluded from the net active calories calculation so they do not inflate your target.

Macronutrient targets

Default macro targets use a 20% protein / 45% carbs / 35% fat split applied to your base calorie goal. All values can be overridden in Settings → Daily Targets.

Protein

Set as a fixed gram amount or as g/kg body weight. 1.2–1.6 g/kg is typical for active people. At 20% of 2150 kcal: 107 g.

Carbohydrates

45% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 242 g

Fat

35% of calories ÷ 9 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 84 g
