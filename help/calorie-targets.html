Calorie targets

How MyTracker sets your daily calorie goal from a static TDEE baseline and a separate deficit.

Your base target (rest day)

If you fill in your profile in Settings → Calories, MyTracker uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to estimate your maintenance TDEE and then applies your chosen deficit.

BMR (male) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
BMR (female) = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

Maintenance TDEE = BMR × activity level multiplier

If you have not filled in your profile, the target defaults to 2150 kcal maintenance and 0 kcal deficit. You can also set a custom target directly in Settings.

Activity level multipliers

LevelMultiplierDescription
Sedentary 1.2xLittle to no movement
Light 1.375xLight daily movement
Moderate 1.55xTypical day with regular movement
Active 1.725xHigh movement or frequent training
Very active 1.9xVery active job or training volume

Deficit mode

When you set a deficit, MyTracker subtracts it from your maintenance TDEE. Workout calories stay informational only.

Net active calories

Active calories are still tracked, but they do not change the day's intake target.

420 kcal gross burn − 62 kcal resting burn (45 min) = 358 kcal added

This keeps your daily target stable. A workout can still inform your activity context, but your intake goal stays based on TDEE and your chosen deficit.

Duplicate workouts (the same activity imported from both Strava and Google Health on the same day) are detected and excluded from the net calories calculation so they do not inflate your target.

Macronutrient targets

Macro targets use a 30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat split applied to your calorie target. The gram values update automatically if your calorie target changes. You can see today's macro breakdown on the ring charts on the Today tab.

Protein

30% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 161 g

Carbohydrates

40% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 215 g

Fat

30% of calories ÷ 9 kcal/g. At 2150 kcal: 72 g
