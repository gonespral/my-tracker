Strava and Google Health

Import activities automatically and optionally push workouts back with calorie data.

Connecting Strava

Go to Settings → Strava and tap Connect. You will be redirected to Strava's authorisation page. Once approved, MyTracker imports your last 100 activities on every load and keeps them in sync.

1
Open Settings → Strava, tap Connect

You are sent to Strava's OAuth page to approve the connection. MyTracker requests read-only access to activities plus write access for pushing workouts back.

2
Activities are imported on every app load

Strava workouts appear in the Workouts tab and count toward your daily calorie target automatically. Duplicate detection prevents the same workout from being counted twice if it also comes from Google Health.

3
Optionally push workouts back to Strava

Workouts you log directly in MyTracker can be pushed to Strava. Enable auto-push in Settings, or tap the push button on an individual workout card.

Calorie spoofing

Strava does not allow calorie data on manually created activities via their API. When you push a workout from MyTracker to Strava, the calorie count would be lost.

Calorie spoofing works around this by encoding your calories as a synthetic average heart rate. MyTracker uploads the activity as a TCX file with that heart rate embedded. Strava reads the heart rate data and calculates its own calorie estimate, which ends up close to your logged value.

Off
Push without calorie spoofing

Activity is pushed as a simple JSON entry. No calorie or heart rate data is included. Strava shows no calorie count for the activity.

On
Push with calorie spoofing

Activity is uploaded as a TCX file with a synthetic heart rate derived from your calories, weight, age, and sex. Strava calculates calories from the heart rate and shows a number close to what you logged.

The heart rate formula

The synthetic HR is estimated using the Keytel (2005) formula, which reverses the relationship between heart rate and calorie expenditure:

calPerMin = calories ÷ durationMin

HR (male) = (calPerMin × 4.184 + 55.097 − 0.199 × weight − 0.202 × age) ÷ 0.631
HR (female) = (calPerMin × 4.184 + 20.402 + 0.126 × weight − 0.074 × age) ÷ 0.447

The result is clamped to 60–200 bpm. If the calculation falls outside that range (for example because the calories or duration look implausible) spoofing is disabled for that upload and the activity is pushed without heart rate data.

Custom Strava credentials

By default MyTracker uses a shared Strava app registration. If you want to use your own Strava API application (for example to remove the shared app name from your Strava activity feed), enable Use custom credentials in Settings and paste your own Client ID and Client Secret.

Google Health

Connect via Settings → Google Health. MyTracker pulls activities from Google Fit and other connected Google Health sources. The same duplicate detection that applies to Strava activities applies here, so workouts that appear in both services are only counted once.

You can enable automatic cross-push: when a new Strava activity is imported, it can be automatically forwarded to Google Health as well. Toggle this in Settings → Strava → Auto-push to Google Health.
