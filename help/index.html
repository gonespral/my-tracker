How MyTracker works and how to get the most out of it.

What is MyTracker?

MyTracker is a personal health dashboard for tracking food, workouts, and weight. Everything is stored in your account and synced across devices. There is no subscription and no ads. Your data belongs to you.

smart_toy

AI-powered logging. Describe a meal or workout in plain language and Claude estimates the numbers. Attach a photo of your plate or speak your log out loud.

local_fire_department

Smart calorie targets. Your target is calculated from your BMR and activity level. On days you exercise it automatically increases by your net calories burned.

schedule

Calorie clock. The triangle on the calorie ring shows where you should be in your eating day based on your meal history across the last 30 days.

directions_bike

Strava and Google Health sync. Import activities automatically and optionally push workouts back with calorie data via a synthetic heart rate.

bar_chart

Nutrition and Workouts tabs. Calorie trends, macro heatmaps, weight history, activity streaks, and weekly summaries all update as you log.

Getting started

1
Sign in with GitHub

Your data is stored in your account and synced across devices. GitHub OAuth is the only sign-in method.

2
Add your Anthropic API key

Go to Settings → Claude and paste your key from console.anthropic.com. The key is stored only on this device and used only to talk to Anthropic's API.

3
Set up your calorie profile (optional)

Go to Settings → Daily Targets and enter your age, sex, height, weight, and activity level. MyTracker will calculate a personalised calorie target.

4
Connect Strava or Google Health (optional)

Go to Settings and connect either service to import your workout history automatically.

5
Start logging

Tap the chat bar and describe what you ate or did. The ring charts on the Today tab update immediately.

Privacy and data

All health data is stored in Supabase with row-level security, meaning only your account can read or write your entries. Your Anthropic API key and any OAuth tokens are stored in localStorage on your device only and are never sent to MyTracker's servers.

MyTracker is open source. The full source code is at github.com/gonespral/my-tracker.
