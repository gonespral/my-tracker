Logging food and workouts

Describe what you ate or did and Claude fills in the numbers.

The chat bar

Everything is logged through the bar at the bottom of the screen. Type a description, attach a photo, or tap the microphone to speak. Claude reads your input and returns structured nutrition or activity data that you can confirm and save.

Food logging

Describe any meal in natural language. Claude estimates calories, protein, carbs, and fat. You can be vague ("a big bowl of pasta") or specific ("200 g chicken breast, grilled"). More detail means more accurate numbers.

1
Claude parses your description

It identifies each food item, estimates a portion size if you did not specify one, then looks up or estimates the macronutrient breakdown.

2
You see a summary card

Calories, protein, carbs, and fat are shown before you confirm. You can edit any value before saving.

3
The entry is saved to your log

It appears in the Today tab under the correct meal slot (breakfast, lunch, snack, or dinner) based on the time you logged it or the meal type you mention.

Meal types

Entries are assigned to breakfast, lunch, snack, or dinner. This matters for two reasons: the macro ring on the Today tab groups by meal, and the calorie clock uses your per-meal history to position the ticker. You can mention the meal type in your message ("had a snack, apple and peanut butter") or let Claude infer it from the time of day.

Workout logging

Describe your activity the same way you would to a friend. Claude detects the activity type, estimates duration, and calculates calories burned.

Example inputs
"45 min easy run, felt good"
"1 hour weightlifting, upper body"
"20 min HIIT, roughly 300 cal"
"swim, 1.5 km in 35 mins"

Activity type detection

MyTracker reads the description and automatically assigns an activity type. This controls the icon shown on the workout card and how the entry is categorised in the Workouts tab.

Presets

Frequently logged meals and workouts can be saved as presets. Tap the bookmark icon after logging to save a preset, then select it from the preset list next time to skip the AI step entirely.
