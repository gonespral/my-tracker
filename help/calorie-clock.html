The Calorie Clock

How the ticker on your calorie ring knows where you are in your day.

Try it — tap meals to log them
0 kcal

What is the clock?

The small triangle on your calorie ring is a progress ticker. It marks where you should be in your eating day based on your meal history. If the ring's filled arc is ahead of the ticker, you are ahead of your typical pace. If it is behind, you have room left for the day.

How the position is calculated

1
Looks at your last 30 days of meals

For each meal type (breakfast, lunch, snack, dinner) it sums the calories you logged and counts how many days you actually ate that meal, skipping days where it was not recorded.

2
Builds a per-meal average that ignores unlogged days

If you only eat lunch on weekdays, the lunch average is calculated over around 22 days, not 30. Days where you did not log a meal do not drag the average down.

3
Each meal gets a calorie weight

The averages are converted into fractions of your total daily intake. For example, 480 kcal breakfast, 620 kcal lunch, 210 kcal snack, 730 kcal dinner gives roughly 23%, 30%, 10%, and 36% of the day.

4
The ticker advances as you log meals

Log breakfast and the ticker jumps to the 23% mark. Log lunch too and it moves to 53%. Log all four and it reaches 100% at the top of the ring.

Three states

Hidden
New user, no history yet

Without enough data to calculate meaningful averages the ticker stays hidden. It appears once your 30-day meal history builds up.

Clock mode
History exists, nothing logged today

The ticker falls back to a time-of-day position, moving linearly from 7 am (0%) to 10 pm (100%). A rough guide for how far through your eating window you are.

Meal mode
Meals logged today

The ticker switches to your historical meal weights. Each logged meal advances it by that meal's calorie proportion. This is the most accurate mode and kicks in as soon as you log your first meal.

Meals you have never logged

If a meal type has no history at all, MyTracker assigns it the average of your other known meals as a stand-in, so the ticker fractions still add up to 100%.

The ticker is a pace guide, not a strict target. The ring always shows your real logged calories accurately. The ticker just reflects typical-you, not today-you.

Why per-meal averages instead of daily totals?

Averaging raw daily calories would be thrown off by days where you only logged one meal. A 600 kcal day might just be an incomplete log, not a real rest day. By computing averages per meal, using only the days each meal was actually logged, MyTracker gets a much more faithful picture of what a typical breakfast or dinner looks like for you.
